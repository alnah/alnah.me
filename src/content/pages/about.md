# About

I help people learn or teach French as a foreign language using technology.

## For you

On this blog, you will find tools, ideas, and practical resources at the intersection of pedagogy, programming, and [agentic workflows](https://simonwillison.net/guides/agentic-engineering-patterns/what-is-agentic-engineering/).

## How I work

My approach is connected to my own experience. Learning American English and Brazilian Portuguese helped me understand the difficulties involved in learning a language. Teaching French daily on [italki](https://www.italki.com/fr/teacher/8123606) keeps me engaged with the challenges my students face. Posting and commenting on [r/learnfrench](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnfrench/) and [r/French](https://www.reddit.com/r/French/) on Reddit helps me stay connected to the language-learning communities. These experiences guide the methods and tools I share here.

## Open tools, commercial services

My [blog content](https://alnah.me) and [open-source tools](https://github.com/alnah) are free for people who want to learn at their own pace and are curious about programming or [agentic workflows](https://simonwillison.net/guides/agentic-engineering-patterns/what-is-agentic-engineering/). I also offer commercial services for people and organizations that need more direct results, or help integrating my free tools into their own ecosystem. ## About me

I'm Alexis Nahan, also known as `alnah`. I'm a French teacher and developer based in Salvador, Brazil. French is my native language, and I teach it daily on [italki](https://www.italki.com/fr/teacher/8123606).

I follow [Eaquals recommendations for scenario-based learning](https://www.eaquals.org/wp-content/uploads/Inventaire_ONLINE_full.pdf), the [input/output model](https://www.britishcouncil.org/voices-magazine/can-we-learn-second-language-we-learned-our-first), and the [CEFR descriptors](https://europass.europa.eu/en/what-common-european-framework-languages-cefr), which I use as a framework for understanding language proficiency.

I connect language teaching with programming. I translate technical possibilities into educational solutions, and I build things that learners, teachers, and organizations can use. Some of my [projects](https://github.com/alnah) include [Falaro](https://github.com/alnah/falaro), and [Picoloom](https://github.com/alnah/picoloom).
